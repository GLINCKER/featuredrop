# The Banner & Toast Components

While FeatureDrop includes modal takeovers like the Announcement Modal, often you simply need to alert the user to a change without blocking their current workflow. For this, we provide the `<Banner>` and `<Toast>` primitives.

## Usage & Strategy

These two components serve distinct psychological roles in an application interface.

### The Banner
Use a `<Banner>` for high-importance, persistent updates that should remain continuously visible until the user explicitly acknowledges them. Banners typically mount flush to the top or bottom of your viewport or a specific container. 

*Example: "Our privacy policy is changing next week. Please review."*

### The Toast
Use a `<Toast>` for lightweight, ephemeral notifications and quick acknowledgements. Toasts typically float in the corner of the screen and automatically dismiss themselves after a few seconds.

*Example: "Dark mode is now available. Try it out!"*

<Callout type="tip">
  **The Staged Launch Strategy**: 
  A common best practice is to combine both components during a staged launch. You can mount a persistent `<Banner>` that warns users of an upcoming deadline for 14 days, and then on launch day, hit them with a celebratory `<Toast>`.
</Callout>
